# White Label CRM for Agencies: What Actually Matters in 2026 (With Real Pricing)

Every agency eventually hits the same wall. You’re managing client leads in spreadsheets, losing follow-ups, and fielding texts that say, *“Did you get my form?”* The fix is a CRM—but clients expect it to look like *your* agency built it, not like you rented software from a third party.

That’s where white-label CRM platforms come in. They promise your logo, your domain, your colors, and your pricing. The reality is messier. Some platforms slap a logo on a login screen and call it white-label. Others let you rebuild the entire experience from the ground up—for a price.

This guide cuts through the marketing. We compare the four most talked-about white-label CRM options for agencies in 2026: **GoHighLevel**, **Vendasta**, **HubSpot**, and **Nida**. No affiliate links. Just actual pricing, real white-label depth, and honest advice on margins.

---

## What "White Label" Actually Means for CRMs

Before comparing platforms, you need to know what you’re shopping for. White-label CRM features usually break down into three tiers:

1. **Skin-deep white-label** — Your logo appears on the login page and in email headers. The URL still says `vendor.com`. Clients know it’s not yours.
2. **Domain-mapped white-label** — The platform lives on your subdomain (e.g., `crm.youragency.com`). Clients see your branding in the URL, emails, and dashboard.
3. **Full white-label** — Your domain, your branding, your pricing inside the platform, custom client onboarding flows, and the ability to hide all vendor references completely.

Most agencies need at least tier 2. If a client googles the platform name and finds a public pricing page undercutting your rates, you’ve lost credibility.

## What Agencies Actually Need (vs. What Vendors Sell)

CRM vendors love to demo features your clients will never use. Here is what actually moves the needle for agencies reselling CRM services:

### 1. Simple Pipeline Visibility

Clients need to see where their leads are without a training manual. A CRM with 47 pipeline stages and custom object fields sounds powerful. For a local plumber or realtor, it’s overwhelming. The best white-label CRM for agencies is the one clients can read in 30 seconds.

### 2. Automated Follow-Up That Works Out of the Box

You should not be writing email sequences from scratch on day one. A reseller-focused platform should include pre-built follow-up automations for common industries (dental, real estate, home services, legal). GoHighLevel calls these *snapshots*. Nida includes them by default. Vendasta offers similar through bundles. HubSpot requires you to build everything.

### 3. Flat Pricing at Scale

Per-seat or per-contact pricing kills margins. If your client’s business grows and their contact list doubles, your CRM cost should not double. Look for unlimited-contact, unlimited-client plans. That is the only way to maintain margin as you scale.

### 4. True White-Label Confidence

If a client can google the platform and find a public pricing page, you have a leak in your business model. Full white-label means *no* vendor branding, *no* public pricing discovery, and *your* domain everywhere.

## Red Flags When Choosing a White-Label CRM

- **No custom domain option.** If the login URL is the vendor’s, you’re not white-labeling—you’re affiliate marketing.
- **Usage-based pricing that spikes.** A CRM should not cost more because your client sent 500 extra emails.
- **Forced marketplace bundles.** If you must buy reputation management, listings, and ad tools just to get the CRM, your margins are being held hostage.
- **No client ownership.** Some platforms let you white-label, but the client relationship belongs to the vendor. If you leave, your clients stay behind.
