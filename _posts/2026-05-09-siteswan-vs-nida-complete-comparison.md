# SiteSwan vs. Nida: A Complete Comparison

If you’re comparing SiteSwan and Nida, you’re probably trying to decide which white-label website platform will power your reseller business. Both are built for agencies and entrepreneurs who want to sell websites without writing code—but they approach the problem differently.

This post compares them directly: features, pricing, strengths, weaknesses, and who each is actually for.

---

## At a Glance

| | SiteSwan | Nida |
|---|---|---|
| **Target user** | Local web design resellers, “churn-and-burn” site sellers | Branded agencies, freelancers, SaaS-style resellers |
| **Hosting model** | Included / bundled | Included with custom domain support |
| **White-label depth** | Moderate (branding options exist) | Full (custom domain, unbranded UI, client billing) |
| **Pricing structure** | Tiered plans; per-site limits on lower tiers | Flat monthly; unlimited sites on most plans |
| **Ease of use** | Very easy | Easy; modern UI |
| **E-commerce support** | Basic | Available on higher tiers |
| **CRM / marketing tools** | Minimal | Built-in lead forms; integrations for CRM |
| **Blog / CMS** | Basic | Yes |
| **SEO tools** | Meta tags, basic settings | Meta tags, Open Graph, redirects, sitemap |
| **Client handoff** | Managed through platform reseller dashboard | Fully branded dashboard you control |
| **Support** | Email / ticket | Email / ticket / docs |

## Pricing Breakdown

| Plan Type | SiteSwan | Nida |
|---|---|---|
| Entry / Starter | ~$59–$99/mo (limited sites) | ~$29/mo |
| Mid-tier | ~$199/mo | ~$49/mo |
| Unlimited / Pro | ~$299+/mo | ~$99/mo (or flat unlimited) |

**Important context:** SiteSwan’s pricing is structured around the number of sites and bundled hosting. Nida uses a flat-rate model: one price, unlimited sites. If you plan to scale past 10–20 clients, the math heavily favors a flat-rate platform.

---

## Who Is SiteSwan For?

SiteSwan is built for the classic “local website guy.” The founder/operator who sells $500–$1,000 websites to dentists, plumbers, and real estate agents in their town. It’s intentionally simple: you pick a template, swap in the client’s info, and go live.

**Strengths:**
- Extremely low learning curve
- Fast time-to-first-site
- Industry-specific templates (great for local niches)
- Established player with a track record

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited flexibility on design and advanced features
- White-label options feel “bolted on” rather than native
- Per-site pricing limits margins as you grow
- Modern web expectations (animations, advanced SEO, integrations) are harder to meet

**Bottom line:** If you want to sell basic local business sites quickly and don’t care about building a long-term brand, SiteSwan works.

---

## Who Is Nida For?

Nida is built for resellers who want to **own the relationship** and **keep the margin**. It’s for freelancers and agencies who say, “This is *my* platform,” not “I use a platform.”

**Strengths:**
- True white-label: your domain, your logo, your pricing
- Flat monthly pricing (unlimited sites on most plans)
- Modern builder with clean code output
- SEO and performance controls that matter for real businesses
- Designed to feel like your own SaaS product

**Weaknesses:**
- Fewer niche templates out of the box (growing library)
- Smaller ecosystem of add-ons than legacy platforms
- As a newer platform, some edge-case features are still rolling out

**Bottom line:** If you want to build a scalable agency brand with healthy margins and a recurring model, Nida is built for that.
