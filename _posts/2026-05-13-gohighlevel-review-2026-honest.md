# GoHighLevel Review 2026: An Honest Look at the Good, Bad, and Ugly

GoHighLevel (GHL) dominates the "marketing platform" conversation. Every YouTube guru calls it the holy grail. But is it?

I spent three years in GoHighLevel. I built funnels, set up automations, white-labeled dashboards for clients, and wrestled with support tickets. Here's the honest truth.

---

## What's Good

### Comprehensive Features

GHL is a Swiss Army knife. CRM, email/SMS marketing, funnels, appointment booking, reputation management, social posting, and course hosting — all in one dashboard. For agencies managing multiple clients, this consolidation is powerful. You don't need five logins.

### White-Label Capability

The white-label option lets you rebrand the platform as your own software. Clients log into *your* URL. This is a genuine competitive advantage and a strong resale play.

### Active Community

The Facebook community is massive. Thousands of agency owners share templates, workflows, and troubleshooting help daily. If you're stuck, someone has probably solved it.

---

## What's Bad

### Complexity That Punishes Beginners

GHL has a brutal learning curve. Setting up a basic automation requires understanding triggers, filters, If/Else branches, time delays, and tag logic. For a solo operator without a tech background, "quick setups" turn into weekend-long rabbit holes.

### Pricing That Penalizes Growth

The Starter plan ($97/mo) is too limited. Most agencies need the Unlimited plan ($297/mo). Add SMS credits, additional phone numbers, email sending limits, premium snapshots, and integrations — you're easily at $400–$600/month before you've closed your third client.

Worse, costs scale with every added sub-account. It's a treadmill.

### Slow, Generic Support

Support is a lottery. Sometimes you get answers in a few hours. Sometimes it's days. The knowledge base is bloated and outdated. Critical fixes often require community workarounds.
