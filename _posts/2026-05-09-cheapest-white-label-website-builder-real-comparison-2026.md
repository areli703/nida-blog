# Cheapest White Label Website Builder in 2026: A Real Cost Comparison

If you’re starting a website reseller business, your platform choice directly determines your margins. A builder that looks cheap at $29/month can quietly cost you hundreds in per-site fees, transaction markups, and mandatory add-ons.

We signed up for six of the most talked-about white-label and reseller-friendly website builders—SiteSwan, Duda, Vendasta, GoHighLevel, Wix Studio, and Nida—and tracked every line item that affects a reseller’s bottom line. Here’s what we found.

---

## What "Cheapest" Actually Means for Resellers

Most reviews compare retail prices. Resellers care about something different: **the total cost to build, host, and manage client sites at scale**.

A platform that charges per published site, for example, gets expensive fast. If you have 20 clients and pay $15/site/month, that’s $300 in platform costs alone—before you’ve paid yourself.

The real metric is **cost per client site per month**, including:

- Platform subscription
- Per-site or per-client fees
- Hosting included vs. extra
- White-label features (sometimes a paid tier)
- E-commerce or booking add-ons
- Support or onboarding costs

## Where the Hidden Costs Hide

### SiteSwan

SiteSwan’s reseller program is flat-rate on its higher plan, but the entry tier can limit the number of active sites. The bigger issue is that **SiteSwan is only a website builder**. If your client later asks for a CRM, email automation, or booking system, you’re buying and integrating separate tools. That integration time—and those extra subscriptions—are real costs.

**Hidden cost:** Tool stack fragmentation. You may end up paying for Zapier, a CRM, and a form builder on top of SiteSwan.

### Duda

Duda is a serious platform with solid white-label options, but the pricing escalates quickly. The $149 tier is white-labeled, but if you want client management tools, priority support, or team seats, you’re pushed toward $300+/month plans.

**Hidden cost:** Team seats and priority support. At agency scale, you may need the $735 tier.

### Vendasta

Vendasta’s website builder is technically free in some bundles, but Vendasta is built as an all-in-one marketplace. You pay for a "package" of tools, and the website builder is just one item in the cart. If you only need websites, you’re subsidizing CRM, reputation management, and ad tools you may not use.

**Hidden cost:** Bundle bloat. It’s hard to buy just the website builder.

### GoHighLevel

GoHighLevel includes unlimited websites and funnels, which sounds like a reseller dream. The catch is that GoHighLevel is primarily a CRM and automation platform. The website builder works, but it’s not as design-flexible as dedicated builders. If a client wants a visually rich custom site, you may still need a second platform.

**Hidden cost:** Design limitations push you toward hiring a designer or using a secondary builder for complex projects.

### Wix Studio

Wix Studio is Wix’s agency-facing product. It has improved white-label options, but it’s still fundamentally Wix—clients know the ecosystem, and true white-labeling (your own domain, your own branding everywhere) requires the higher agency tiers.

**Hidden cost:** Brand leakage. Clients may recognize the Wix backend, which undermines the "built by my agency" positioning.

### Nida

Nida’s pricing is flat: $29–$99/month for unlimited sites, white-labeling, hosting, CRM, automations, and chatbot tools all in one. There are no per-site fees and no forced bundles. The trade-off is that Nida is newer and has a smaller template library than Duda or SiteSwan.

**Hidden cost:** Fewer pre-built niche templates. You may spend slightly more time on initial setup for unusual industries.

## When "Cheapest" Isn’t the Right Choice

Price is only one variable. Here are three scenarios where paying more makes sense:

1. **You need 200+ niche templates today.** SiteSwan and Duda have deeper libraries. If your entire pitch is "I can launch your restaurant site in 24 hours," those templates matter.

2. **You’re already committed to GoHighLevel’s CRM.** If your agency is built inside GoHighLevel for automation and pipelines, using its built-in website builder keeps your stack simple—even if the design tools are weaker.

3. **You want a bundled marketplace.** Vendasta makes sense if you genuinely want to resell reputation management, listings, and ad services alongside websites. The bundle can be convenient, even if it’s not the cheapest way to buy just websites.
