## What Is SiteSwan?

SiteSwan markets itself as a **white-label website reseller program** — a complete business-in-a-box for people who want to build and sell websites to local small businesses without coding.

The pitch is compelling: you get a white-label platform, pre-built themes, a marketing toolkit, and training. You sell websites under your own brand. SiteSwan runs invisibly behind the scenes.

After spending a week inside the platform and talking to three active resellers, here's what we found.

## What SiteSwan Does Well

**1. True White-Label Experience**

Everything your client sees carries your branding. The builder interface, client dashboard, and even the domain infrastructure can be white-labeled. This isn't a "powered by SiteSwan" badge situation.

**2. Pre-Built Industry Themes**

SiteSwan offers 100+ templates organized by industry: restaurants, contractors, dentists, real estate, lawyers, salons. For resellers who want to launch fast, this saves hours.

**3. One-Page Pricing Setup**

SiteSwan uses a flat monthly licensing model. You pay your fee, you get access to everything. You set your own prices for clients. Many resellers charge $1,500-$3,000 for a website build plus $50-$150/month for hosting.

## Where SiteSwan Falls Short

**1. No CRM or Marketing Automation**

SiteSwan is **just a website builder**. If you want to offer a complete business system to your clients, you're still buying and integrating additional tools.

**2. Limited Design Flexibility**

The themes are good starting points but they're templates. If a client wants something truly custom — advanced animations, custom functionality — you're working within SiteSwan's constraints.

**3. Mobile Responsiveness Issues**

While SiteSwan claims mobile-responsive themes, we found that complex customizations often break mobile layouts. Resellers reported spending extra time testing and fixing mobile views.

**4. Not Passive Income**

SiteSwan includes training and marketing materials — all valuable. But the actual fulfillment is still on you. You need to do the selling, manage the client relationship, and handle ongoing support.

## Who SiteSwan Is For

SiteSwan makes sense if:
- You want to sell basic informational websites to local businesses
- You don't need CRM, automation, or advanced marketing tools
- You're comfortable with template-based design
- Your clients primarily need brochure sites

SiteSwan is NOT ideal if:
- You want to sell complete business systems (websites + CRM + automation + chatbot)
- Your clients need advanced features (custom apps, complex booking, e-commerce)
- You want a single platform that handles everything

## SiteSwan Pricing Breakdown

- **Monthly license fee**: Varies by plan
- **Your client pricing**: You set this ($1,500-$3,000 upfront + $50-$150/month typical)
- **Your profit margin**: Depends on pricing and client volume

## The Verdict

SiteSwan delivers on its core promise: a white-label website builder for resellers. The themes are decent, the white-labeling is genuine, and the training materials are solid.

But if your goal is to build a recurring revenue agency by selling **complete business systems**, you'll find yourself duct-taping multiple tools together.

**Grade: B+** — Excellent for pure website reselling. Not a complete agency platform.
