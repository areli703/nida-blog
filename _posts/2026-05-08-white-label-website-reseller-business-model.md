# The Economics of a White-Label Website Reseller Business

Starting a website reseller business is one of the lowest-overhead ways to build a real agency. You don’t need a design degree, a dev team, or a downtown office. But low overhead doesn’t mean “no math.” If you want predictable profit, you need to understand the economics.

This post breaks down:

- Real startup costs
- Monthly vs. one-time pricing models
- Break-even math
- Margin scenarios at three agency sizes

## Startup Costs (Month 1)

| Cost Item | Estimated Price |

Compared to opening a coffee shop or buying a franchise, this is pocket change. The real investment is your time.

## Two Ways to Charge Clients

### Model A: One-Time Build + Hosting

You charge a flat fee to build the site, then a smaller annual or monthly hosting/maintenance fee.

| Line Item | Example Price |
|-----------|---------------|
| Website build (5-page site) | $800–$2,500 |
| Hosting + maintenance/yr | $200–$600 |

**Pros:** Large upfront cash. Easy for clients to understand.  
**Cons:** Income stops after the build unless you upsell. “Feast or famine” cash flow.

### Model B: Monthly Subscription (All-Inclusive)

The client pays one monthly fee that covers the build, hosting, updates, and support.

| Tier | Monthly Price | What’s Included |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| Starter | $99–$149/mo | 5-page site, hosting, basic edits |
| Growth | $199–$299/mo | 10-page site, blog, SEO setup, edits |
| Premium | $399–$599/mo | E-commerce, email support, content updates |

**Pros:** Predictable recurring revenue. Higher lifetime value (LTV). Easier to sell a “package.”  
**Cons:** You don’t get a big payday upfront. You must deliver ongoing value.

## Break-Even Math

Here’s what it takes to cover your costs and start paying yourself.

### Assumptions

- Platform cost: **$49/mo**
- Tools (email, scheduler, etc.): **$25/mo**
- Your target draw/salary: **$3,000/mo**
- Average client value (monthly model): **$150/mo**

| Metric | Calculation | Result |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Total monthly overhead | $49 + $25 | **$74/mo** |
| Clients to cover overhead | $74 ÷ $150 | **1 client** |
| Clients to hit $3k salary | ($3,000 + $74) ÷ $150 | **~21 clients** |

At **21 clients** paying $150/mo, you’re making $3,150/mo in revenue and netting roughly $3,076 after platform costs. That’s $36,000+ annually from a business that costs almost nothing to run.

If you use a **one-time model** and charge $1,200 per build, you need **3 builds per month** to clear $3,000—and then you have to find new clients every month to maintain it.

## Hidden Costs to Watch

- **Chargebacks / refunds:** Price fairly and set clear expectations.
- **Time spent on revisions:** Bundle edit limits into your packages.
- **Churn:** Losing 2 clients/month hurts more at 10 clients than at 60. Build stickiness (SEO reports, hosting lock-in).
- **Support burden:** As you grow, consider hiring a VA or part-time support person before you burn out.

## Which Model Wins?

| Factor | One-Time Build | Monthly Subscription |
|--------|--------------|----------------------|
| Cash flow | Lumpy | Predictable |
| Client LTV | Lower | 3–10× higher |
| Scalability | Poor | Excellent |
| Stress level | Higher | Lower |
| Sale value of agency | Lower | Higher (recurring revenue multiples) |

Most serious resellers **hybridize**: charge a modest setup fee ($200–$500) plus a monthly plan. This gives you some upfront cash while building a durable revenue stream.
